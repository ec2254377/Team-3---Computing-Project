ECO-FRIENDLY PRODUCTION



There are many ways of dealing with food sustainably. Let's look through some of the top methods.

Reducing Prices Near Expiry

Companies employ markdown strategies to offer discounts on items nearing expiration, encouraging swift sales and minimizing waste. These discounts, often up to 50%, make products more accessible to consumers while preventing spoilage and financial losses for businesses.

Surprise Bags and Apps

Platforms like TooGoodToGo facilitate the sale of surplus food through surprise bags, fostering affordability and sustainability. For instance, bakeries like Greggs offer assorted baked goods at reduced prices via the app, catering to diverse dietary preferences and expanding access to discounted food options.

Recycling for Fertiliser

Unsalvageable food waste undergoes composting to produce nutrient-rich fertiliser, benefiting agricultural practices and promoting soil health. This sustainable recycling process diverts organic waste from landfills and supports eco-friendly farming techniques.

Energy Production

Food waste serves as a valuable resource for energy generation through methods like anaerobic digestion and biomass conversion. By harnessing biogas from organic waste, companies contribute to renewable energy production while mitigating environmental impacts associated with traditional waste disposal methods.

Feed for Animals

In regions where regulations allow, repurposing food waste as animal feed minimises waste and supplements animal diets, contributing to a more circular economy and sustainable agricultural practices.

OUR GLOBAL IMPACT


We promote Earthly Eats, where we dedicate our time towards reducing food waste and any adverse effects across the planet, we have years not decades to conserve our planet and natural resources. Food Waste Management is a pressing concern that we are committed to reducing.



Supply Chain Optimization

One of the key areas where we've made significant changes is in optimizing our supply chain. By improving forecasting accuracy and inventory management, we've reduced the amount of food that goes to waste due to overstocking or expiration. Additionally, we've established partnerships with local farmers and producers to source fresh, seasonal ingredients.


Menu Innovation

We've also focused on menu innovation to utilize ingredients more efficiently. Our culinary team works tirelessly to develop creative dishes that make use of all parts of the ingredients, from root to stem. Through careful menu planning and portion control, we've been able to minimize plate waste while still providing our customers with delicious and satisfying meals.


Employee Training and Engagement

Our commitment to sustainability extends to our employees as well. We provide comprehensive training on food handling, storage, and waste reduction practices to all staff members. Furthermore, we actively encourage employee engagement and participation in sustainability initiatives. From composting programs to energy-saving measures, every team member plays a role in our sustainability efforts.
